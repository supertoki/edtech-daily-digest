Key Themes & Trends

1. Governance Before Procurement

The clearest pattern of the week: set the rules first. EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," EdTech Magazine's analysis of SUNY's systemwide AI policy deadline, and the Harvard-sourced principal's guide all argue that policy and governance frameworks must precede tool adoption — a maturing of the earlier "talk first" framing into formal institutional structure.

2. Cybersecurity Turns Offensive-Aware

Threats are now AI-powered and the defense posture is shifting accordingly: AI phishing targeting K-12 data, campuses ramping up deepfake defenses, the post-Canvas-breach vendor-risk reckoning (275M users exposed), and THE Journal's reframing of AI in security from finding flaws to fixing them. 1EdTech's "shared responsibility" webinar is the policy follow-on.

3. ISTE Rebrands as ISTELive Convenes

ISTE+ASCD has become the International Society for Transforming Education, dropping the combined acronym — landing as ISTELive 26 draws attendees to "explore the future of learning." A significant identity shift for one of edtech's anchor organizations.

4. Credentials & Apprenticeships Stay Central

Inside Higher Ed's apprenticeship coverage ("The Apprenticeship Wish List," rural pathways) and EdSurge's degrees-in-the-age-of-AI piece sustain the running argument over what proves skill — with calls for funding, legal updates, and better data to scale work-based learning.

5. The Cost & Realism Check on AI

THE Journal's "Half of Gen AI Projects Could Exceed Budget by 2028" and Brookings' "Risks of Anthropomorphic AI" inject a sober counterweight to adoption enthusiasm — budgets, evidence, and the hazards of human-like AI in classrooms all under fresh scrutiny.

6. Data Foundations & Equity (Carryover)

Ed-Fi's fresh impact stories (Indiana readiness tracking, early literacy, enrollment/funding) and Brookings' Zanzibar engagement work keep data infrastructure and global equity in view beneath the AI headlines.
