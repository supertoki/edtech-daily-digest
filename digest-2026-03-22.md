## Key Themes & Trends

### 1. AI Everywhere -- But Growing Pains Are Real
AI dominates the conversation across nearly every source. From shadow AI cybersecurity risks (THE Journal), to families' first impressions (EdTech Digest), to students seeking AI help on social media rather than through schools (Inside Higher Ed), the narrative is shifting from "should we use AI?" to "how do we manage AI that's already here?" The DOL's new AI Literacy Framework signals that workforce readiness now formally includes AI competency.

### 2. Digital Equity and the Connectivity Divide
Multiple sources highlight that AI-powered learning tools are only as good as the infrastructure supporting them. EdTech Digest's piece on reimagining access and EdSurge's coverage of screen-free school legislation represent two sides of the same coin: ensuring technology serves all students equitably, whether through better connectivity or more intentional usage policies.

### 3. Cybersecurity as a First-Order Concern
Shadow AI, network security guides, and data protection announcements all point to cybersecurity becoming a top-tier priority for K-12 and higher ed IT teams. The proliferation of unapproved AI tools in classrooms is creating new attack surfaces that existing policies were not designed to address.

### 4. Career Pathways and Workforce Alignment
EdSurge, 1EdTech, and multiple sources emphasize employer-education collaboration. Whether it is co-designing cybersecurity curricula or building credential recognition frameworks, the edtech sector is increasingly oriented around workforce outcomes rather than purely academic metrics.

### 5. Human-Centered Design and the Screen Time Debate
A philosophical counter-current is emerging: multiple pieces question whether more technology is always better. From restoring childhood wonder in screen-filled worlds to emphasizing human problem-solving skills alongside tech advancement, the field is wrestling with balance.

### 6. Data-Driven Student Support
Ed-Fi's impact stories and EdTech Magazine's coverage of AI attendance tracking highlight how real-time data dashboards and predictive analytics are being used to combat chronic absenteeism, support early literacy, and smooth student transfers.
