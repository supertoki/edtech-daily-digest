Key Themes & Trends

1. Govern Before You Buy

A striking cluster of coverage insists policy must precede procurement: EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools" and the Harvard/MIT cautions, SUNY's new systemwide AI policy, and 1EdTech's "Trusted Learning Context" public comment all treat AI adoption as a governance question first. The message: buying tools before writing rules inverts the order.

2. Skepticism About AI's Human Costs Sharpens

Inside Higher Ed's call for "Human Intelligence Labs," Brookings' "5 Lessons from Teachers on the Risks of Anthropomorphic AI," and EdSurge's "Can an Algorithm Replace a Teacher's Instinct?" converge on one worry — that human-like AI erodes the human judgment and connection at the center of teaching.

3. Data Foundations and the ROI Reckoning

THE Journal anchors the pragmatic thread: AI impact "relies on a strong data foundation," half of gen-AI projects "could exceed budget by 2028," and workloads are shifting to private cloud. Enthusiasm is giving way to questions of cost, infrastructure, and measurable return.

4. The Canvas Breach Keeps Reshaping Vendor Risk

EdTech Magazine's "Manage Vendor Risk After the Canvas Breach" and 1EdTech's "Shared Responsibility" webinar keep vendor accountability and shared-security models on the agenda weeks after the incident.

5. An Industry Consolidating and Rebranding

ISTE+ASCD's rebrand to the International Society for Transforming Education (with an expanded "AI-Ready Graduate" framework) and ETS's acquisition of ACT signal structural realignment — assessment and standards organizations repositioning around competency and AI readiness.

6. Workforce and Infrastructure Undercurrents

Marshall University's cybersecurity-workforce pipeline, E-Rate advocacy at ISTELive 26, and the looming Chromebook-to-"Googlebooks" transition keep the unglamorous but consequential questions of funding, hardware, and talent in frame.
