Executive Summary

Security is the dominant chord today, but it has shifted from "lock the doors" to defending against synthetic media: EdTech Magazine runs parallel K-12 and higher-ed pieces on deepfakes, AI phishing, and cyber resilience, while THE Journal reports AI is moving cybersecurity from finding flaws to fixing them. Underneath sits a quieter human thread: Brookings warns about anthropomorphic AI, Inside Higher Ed argues "We Have Never Taught Critical Thinking," and an EdSurge teacher recounts pulling his AI grading tool after it praised a student in words he never wrote.

Key Themes & Trends

1. Security Pivots to Synthetic Media

The week's clearest pattern is deepfakes and AI-powered attacks moving to center stage. EdTech Magazine pairs K-12 ("Deepfakes in Education," "AI Phishing Gains Inside Access") with higher ed ("Institutions Ramp Up Defenses Against Deepfakes," "Building Cyber Resilience"), while THE Journal reports AI is shifting cybersecurity from finding flaws to fixing them. The threat surface is now AI-generated — and so is the defense.

2. Govern Before You Buy

A remarkably consistent message across the startup and practitioner press: set the rules first. EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," the Harvard principal's guide, and MIT's Justin Reich ("What Schools Still Don't Know About AI") all argue for governance and skepticism ahead of procurement — reinforced by 1EdTech's June 25 "shared responsibility" security webinar.

3. Data Readiness as the AI Prerequisite

Higher-ed IT coverage converges on a single foundation: "Why Data Readiness Is the Foundation for AI Readiness," "Data Governance Is Just the Beginning," and Ventura College's faculty AI-readiness communities of practice all frame clean, governed, well-understood data as the thing that must come before any AI return.

4. The Human-Accountability Counterweight

Against the adoption push, several pieces insist on human judgment: EdSurge's AI-grading-tool confession (an educator pulled the feature after it spoke for him), EdTech Digest's "I Almost Let AI Take My Voice," and Brookings' "Risks of Anthropomorphic AI" all warn against ceding too much to systems that imitate people.

5. The Cognition Question Persists

Inside Higher Ed's "We Have Never Taught Critical Thinking" reframes the AI panic as a mirror — AI exposes pedagogical gaps that predate it — while "AI Across the Curriculum" documents institutions trying to teach with it deliberately. The debate over what AI does to thinking remains unresolved and active.

6. Conference Season Builds

ISTELive 26 (Orlando) leads the near-term calendar, with EdTech Week (Oct, NYC), 1EdTech's Learning Impact Europe (Sep, Thessaloniki) and CBExchange (Sep–Oct) on the horizon — and Learning Impact 2027 dates now posted for San Antonio.
