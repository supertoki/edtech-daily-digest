Key Themes & Trends

1. From Adoption to Accountability — Prove the ROI

EdTech Magazine's cluster (Cybersecurity ROI, Data Literacy = AI ROI, K-12's "From Acquisition to Accountability") signals the market has moved past the adoption debate. Leaders now have to justify spend with measurable returns, and the budget conversation is the new battleground.

2. AI Readiness Rests on a Data Foundation

The Fabric/Purview readiness guide, the OneRoster/Ed-Fi interoperability webinar, Ed-Fi's impact stories, and THE Journal's "AI Is Moving Faster than Data Trust" all reinforce a single message: trustworthy AI is impossible without governed, interoperable data plumbing underneath it.

3. The Humanist Counter-Current

EdSurge's "Teach Humanity Before It Disappears," ISTE on human problem-solving, and Inside Higher Ed's writing-vs-thinking and "I Quit the LMS" columns form a coherent pushback against tech maximalism — preserving human judgment, connection, and intentionality as AI scales.

4. Compliance Becomes Concrete

ADA Title II web-accessibility guidance for small districts and 1EdTech's "shared responsibility" security webinar show governance descending from principle into the specific, deadline-driven obligations that practitioners actually have to meet.

5. Universities as Counterweight

Joshua Kim's column positions higher ed as an institutional check on Silicon Valley's AI narrative — a notable pivot from "how do we adopt" toward "what should we resist and reframe."
