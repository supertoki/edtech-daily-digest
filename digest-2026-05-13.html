Key Themes & Trends

1. Canvas Crisis — Operational Consequences Become Visible

Inside Higher Ed's "Universities Suspend Final Exams After Canvas Hack" (May 8) is the operational-reality shoe to drop after the ransom news. Paired with EdTech Magazine's K-12 and Higher Ed companion pieces on third-party SaaS risk (both new this week), the editorial moment has matured: the question has moved from "what happened?" (week of May 8) to "what was the ransom?" (May 11–12) to "how do we operate and procure differently now?" (May 13). EdSurge's May 12 institutional framing — "Latest Canvas Attack Shows Schools Still Struggle With Cybersecurity" — confirms cross-publication editorial alignment.

2. New Leadership Frame — COLO Rises as CTO Authority is Questioned

Inside Higher Ed's May 13 "Leadership Vision of the COLO to Shape Higher Ed Future?" is the editorial pivot to watch. The Chief Online Learning Officer role gets elevated in the same week the CTO is under AI-ROI scrutiny (May 12 survey). The implicit argument: online-learning leadership requires pedagogical, not just technical, authority — and that authority is migrating to a new title.

3. SaaS Procurement Discipline as the Post-Breach Operating Model

EdTech Magazine's paired pieces frame the actionable response: "map data in SaaS systems and connected infrastructure." This dovetails with this week's 1EdTech accessibility-vendor-questions webinar (May 12) and EdSurge's edtech-vetting-backlash piece (May 7). The post-breach operating model is now legibly a procurement-discipline conversation, not a security-tooling conversation.

4. AI ROI Doubt Continues — Now a Category, Not a Story

Inside Higher Ed's May 12 CTO survey produced two distinct articles ("Half of Campus Tech Leaders Question AI's ROI" + "Survey: CTOs on Cybersecurity, AI, the LMS") — the editorial weight signals that "AI ROI scrutiny" is moving from a story to a category. THE Journal's enterprise-AI procurement stack (Gemini Enterprise Agent, MS–OpenAI revised alliance, CSA agentic-AI governance) remains the supply-side counterweight.

5. AI Literacy Frame Adds a Generation-AI Lens

EdTech Digest's "If Students Are Growing Up With AI, Shouldn't They Understand How It Works?" (May 1) pairs with Brookings' "Generation AI Starts Early" (April 1) to introduce a new framing: AI literacy is becoming a curricular question, not just a procurement question — and it now reaches down into early childhood.

6. EdTech Week 2026 Application Window Closing — 6 Days

Speaker applications close May 19, 2026. Final week to be on the program for the October 13–15 Columbia/Teachers College event.

7. Higher Ed Confidence Counterweight Holds

Earlier-week Inside Higher Ed pieces — "Adult Learners Are the New Norm" and "Even as AI Use Spreads, Graduates Maintain Their Degree Was Worth It" — continue as confidence counterweights to the breach-and-ROI-doubt narrative. The credential-value frame is intact even as the tech-investment frame is questioned.

8. IES Reform Surfaces — Research-to-Practice Pipeline Frame

Brookings' "Rebuilding IES to Amplify the Impact of Education Research on Student Outcomes" (April 14) is a research-policy piece worth tracking as IES reform proposals develop in 2026 — directly relevant to data-quality and evidence-base questions across the rest of the sector.
