Key Themes & Trends

1. The Cognition Debate Gets Measured

Brookings escalates the week's recurring AI-and-the-mind thread from pedagogy to metrics: Rebecca Winthrop's "Is It Time to Measure Cognitive Stunting?" asks whether we can quantify AI's effect on developing minds, paired with EdSurge's "I'm Trying to Teach Humanity Before It Disappears." The question is shifting from what AI does to thinking to how we'd measure and prove it.

2. AI Regulation Takes Shape

Gaia Bernstein's "From Bans to Recalls" proposes a public-health framework for AI companion bots — a concrete regulatory model — while 1EdTech's "Trusted Learning Context" public comment and THE Journal's "AI Faster Than Data Trust" survey push governance and data-trust infrastructure to the foreground.

3. The Human & Wellbeing Thread Strengthens

A new EdTech Magazine SEL-technology guide for K-12 leaders, EdTech Digest's "Where Edtech Meets Human Connection," and the recess-revival coverage keep student wellbeing and belonging in view alongside the models-and-frameworks discourse.

4. Credentials as Currency Holds

Inside Higher Ed's Coursera-survey microcredentials story continues to lead, reinforcing EdSurge's "Why College Degrees Matter in the Age of AI" — a sustained argument over what counts as proof of skill as AI reshapes hiring.

5. Security & Data Stay in ROI Mode

EdTech Magazine's cybersecurity-ROI and data-literacy pieces, the Fabric/Purview readiness guide, and 1EdTech's June 25 "shared responsibility" webinar keep the security conversation anchored on justification, measurement, and vendor accountability rather than alarm.
