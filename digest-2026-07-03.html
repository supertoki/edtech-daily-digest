Key Themes & Trends

1. ISTELive 26 Sets the K-12 Agenda

The conference is driving a fresh wave of practical K-12 guidance — building "safer, smarter digital ecosystems," balancing ed-tech tools against core skills, and advocating for E-Rate under federal review. Conference-season coverage now dominates the K-12 feed.

2. Higher-Ed Cybersecurity Surges

A cluster of new higher-ed pieces — defending against deepfakes, building campus "cyber resilience," and managing vendor risk after the Canvas breach — keeps security at the top of the IT-leadership agenda weeks after the incident.

3. Govern Before You Buy

THE Journal's "3 Starting Points for AI Guardrails," EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," and SUNY's systemwide AI policy all insist that policy must precede procurement.

4. AI's Human Costs Stay in Focus

Inside Higher Ed's "Human Intelligence Labs," EdSurge's "Can an Algorithm Replace a Teacher's Instinct?," and Brookings' new "AI Slop, Young Children" event converge on a shared worry that AI erodes human judgment and connection.

5. Data and Workforce Alignment

Ed-Fi's attendance, readiness, and student-transfer stories, alongside EdTech Magazine's "Community Colleges Can Use Data to Align Curriculum With Workforce Needs," reinforce that data infrastructure is increasingly framed around workforce outcomes.

6. Consolidation and Rebrands

ISTE+ASCD's rebrand to the International Society for Transforming Education (with an expanded "AI-Ready Graduate" framework) and ETS's acquisition of ACT signal continued structural realignment across assessment and standards organizations.
