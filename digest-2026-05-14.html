Key Themes & Trends

1. Online-Learning Consolidation — Coursera × Udemy Merger Closes

Inside Higher Ed reports the Coursera–Udemy merger has been completed today (May 14) — the most significant consolidation in the MOOC/online-learning sector in years. This reshapes the supplier landscape institutions buy from and lands precisely when higher ed is also re-evaluating AI procurement and SaaS exposure. Worth watching: contract renegotiations, course-library overlap rationalization, and whether the combined entity pivots toward credential-and-skills or stays course-marketplace.

2. AI Literacy Reframed — "Evaluate the Outputs," Not "Stop Using It"

Two pieces landing this week shift the AI-literacy conversation from access/use to critical evaluation. Inside Higher Ed's "Real Problem With AI in STEM Education" (May 14) argues the issue is "lack of preparation to evaluate AI's outputs," not student use. EdTech Digest's "Access to Struggle" (May 13) makes the complementary developmental argument — early learners are losing productive struggle to AI assistance. Together they extend the AI-literacy framing from last week's "If Students Are Growing Up With AI…" piece and Brookings' "Generation AI Starts Early."

3. AI as Force-Multiplier, Not Substitute — Structured Literacy

THE Journal's May 13 piece "AI Won't Replace Teachers — But It May Be What Makes Structured Literacy Work at Scale" is the day's clearest pro-AI institutional argument — AI as the operational infrastructure that lets evidence-based literacy methods reach every student. Pairs interestingly with Ed-Fi's "Early Literacy Support That Reaches Every Student" impact story: same goal, different stack.

4. Enrollment Decline Joins SaaS Risk as a CIO Pressure

EdTech Magazine's new K-12 piece "5 Ways K-12 IT Leaders Can Prepare for Enrollment Declines" surfaces a fiscal pressure that's now structural, not cyclical. Combined with the AI-ROI doubt from this week's CTO survey and the lingering Canvas-breach procurement-discipline frame, K-12 and higher-ed IT leaders are facing a three-front compression: fewer students → less revenue → harder ROI math → more SaaS scrutiny.

5. Canvas Crisis Receding from Active News

The Canvas storyline has moved out of the daily news cycle (no new May 14 coverage from the major outlets) and into the operating-model frame: EdTech Magazine's paired K-12/Higher Ed SaaS-risk pieces remain the actionable artifacts. Expect this to resurface as institutions begin reporting procurement-process changes.

6. New Leadership Frames Continue to Surface

Inside Higher Ed's May 13 "Leadership Vision of the COLO" (Chief Online Learning Officer) and today's "Empire of AI, AGI and the University" both signal that higher-ed leadership taxonomy is being rewritten in real time. The COLO frame elevates pedagogical authority over technical authority — and today's piece questions whether leadership is even asking the right AI questions at all.

7. EdTech Week 2026 — Speaker Application Window Closes May 19 (5 Days)

Last call to submit speaker applications for the October 13–15 NYC event at Columbia/Teachers College.

8. Student-Success Operationalization — APLU's "Coordinated, Data-Driven Strategies"

Inside Higher Ed's "How Colleges Are Rethinking Student Success" (May 14) reports the APLU shift from siloed initiatives to integrated data-driven student-success operations — directly aligned with Ed-Fi's multi-state impact-story frame (Indiana, Texas, South Carolina) on the K-12 side.
