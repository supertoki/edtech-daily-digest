Key Themes & Trends

1. Agentic AI Forces an Assessment Rethink

Inside Higher Ed's same-day "5 Tips for Assignment Design in the Agentic AI Age" is the signature piece, arriving exactly as THE Journal reports Google moving AI agents into its mainstream product portfolio and survey data confirms 80% of teachers now use AI tools. The throughline: once agents can complete the homework, the assignment is the thing that has to change — pushing educators toward authentic, process-oriented assessment. Inside Higher Ed's "How to Create University Assessments That Serve Learning" reinforces the same pivot on the higher-ed side.

2. Recess Becomes Policy

A rare two-source convergence: Brookings (May 28) and EdSurge (May 27) both spotlight recess within 24 hours of each other. EdSurge documents the on-the-ground push to bring recess back; Brookings's Allee and Hirsh-Pasek make the research-and-policy case that recess is "crucial for education outcomes." Together they mark the well-being / anti-screen backlash maturing from sentiment (the Surgeon General screen-time advisory) into concrete policy advocacy.

3. The Value-of-the-Degree Debate

EdSurge's "Why College Degrees Matter in the Age of AI" (May 28) and Inside Higher Ed's same-day story on UC math professors petitioning to bring back the SAT/ACT reopen the credentialism question from two angles — the worth of the degree itself and the signals used to gate entry. Both surface as AI destabilizes assumptions about what skills and credentials actually certify.

4. SaaS & Cybersecurity Risk Persists

EdTech Magazine's "The Canvas Breach: Reframing Higher Ed's SaaS Risk Exposure" carries the LMS-breach story into higher education, joining the CoSN report (cyber and data privacy as the top AI concerns), the cloud-security "all hands on deck" piece, and zero-trust networking coverage. 1EdTech's queued June 25 "shared responsibility for edtech security" webinar is the natural next beat.

5. AI vs. Human Connection

The persistent counter-current continues: Inside Higher Ed asks "Why Are Students Opening Up to AI Instead of People?", EdTech Digest pairs tech with "human connection" and warns about "the most dangerous thing an AI can say to your kid," and Brookings proposes a public-health recall framework for AI companion bots. The well-being backlash (recess, screen-time advisory) runs alongside it.

6. Learning Impact T-3 Days

The June 1–3 1EdTech conference in San Francisco is now three days out, landing amid the security, AI-governance, and assessment threads. Watch for vendor statements on shared responsibility, AI safety frameworks, and interoperability commitments from the floor.
