Key Themes & Trends

1. AI-Readiness Gets Formalized

The rebranded International Society for Transforming Education is expanding its "AI-Ready Graduate" framework (EdSurge) — the definitional, standards-level version of a shift showing up on the ground in Santa Clara's hands-on "AI Kitchen" and a Texas law dean's return to Socratic teaching (both Inside Higher Ed).

2. Cybersecurity as Operations — Now in K-12

The maturation from alarm to durable practice is spreading downward: K-12 is adopting CTEM, digital hall passes, and physical-security upgrades (EdTech Magazine K-12) while higher ed builds cyber resilience, manages post-Canvas-breach vendor risk, and defends against deepfakes.

3. Governance Before Deployment

EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," the HBS principal's guide, SUNY's systemwide AI policy, and THE Journal's "AI Impact Relies on a Strong Data Foundation" all push the same sequence: policy and data first, procurement second.

4. Credentials & Pathways Stay Contested

Faculty unions opposing three-year degrees, the apprenticeship "wish list" of funding and legal barriers, community colleges aligning curriculum to workforce data, and a call for a university "R&D lab" for academic programs keep the "what counts as proof of skill" debate live.

5. The Learner-Centered Reframe

EdTech Digest's "EdTech Pretends to Be for Students," "vibe coding" sparking reading (EdSurge), and "I Almost Let AI Take My Voice" all argue for putting learners' agency and originality back at the center of AI adoption.

6. EdTech Goes Global

Brookings' upcoming South and Southeast Asia scaling event, its Malawi research, and 1EdTech's Learning Impact Europe convening point to a widening international lens on sustainable edtech.
