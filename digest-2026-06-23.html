Key Themes & Trends

1. Govern Before You Buy

The AI conversation has shifted from adoption to prerequisites. EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools" and the HBS principal's guide pair with EdTech Magazine's "Data Readiness Is the Foundation for AI Readiness" and the Ventura College faculty-capacity model — a consistent message that governance, data hygiene, and human readiness must precede procurement.

2. AI's Threat Surface Widens

The dark side of classroom AI is now front-page: an "agentic AI cheating crisis" (EdTech Magazine, Inside Higher Ed honor codes), AI-powered phishing and deepfakes targeting K–12 data and students, and a security posture shifting "from finding flaws to fixing them" (THE Journal). 1EdTech's June 25 shared-responsibility webinar speaks directly to who owns the risk.

3. The AI Cost Reckoning

THE Journal's forecast that half of generative-AI projects could exceed budget by 2028 injects fiscal realism into the hype, reinforcing the ROI and "what schools still don't know" framing across EdTech Magazine and EdTech Digest.

4. Credential Honesty

Inside Higher Ed's call for an "honest definition of 'professional certificate'" and EdSurge's "Why College Degrees Matter in the Age of AI" continue the running argument over what counts as credible proof of skill as AI reshapes hiring.

5. Conference Season Turns

ISTELive 26 convenes in Orlando (EdTech Magazine), while the fall calendar fills out — 1EdTech's Learning Impact Europe and CBExchange, and EdTech Week's October program at Columbia — keeping interoperability and startup-investment threads in view.

6. The Human & Equity Thread

Brookings keeps people at the center — teacher support as the starting point for AI in crises, girls' agency out of school in Nigeria, and education systems designed for "human flourishing" — alongside EdSurge's harder questions about whether education truly equalizes.
