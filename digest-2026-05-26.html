Executive Summary

Key Themes & Trends

1. Canvas Breach → SaaS Architecture Reckoning

The story has decisively moved past "what happened" into "how do we architect against this." EdTech Magazine produced near-mirror K-12 and higher-ed pieces reframing the breach as a third-party SaaS risk-mapping exercise. Inside Higher Ed published two opinion pieces in three days (Anderson/Martin on contingency, Skallerup Bessette on lessons). 1EdTech has a shared-responsibility webinar queued for June 25. The next move in this thread will likely come from CISA or state CIOs.

2. AI-Harm-as-Public-Health Frame Consolidating

Three independent signals are aligning: Brookings (Bernstein) explicitly proposing a "bans to recalls" framework for AI companion bots; EdTech Digest's "Most Dangerous Thing an AI Can Say to Your Kid" hitting the same emotional-manipulation threshold; and the U.S. Surgeon General's screen-time advisory. This is a coordinated reframing — AI harm as measurable, regulable product defect rather than parenting problem.

3. Districts Becoming Builders

EdTech Digest's "Vibe Coding" piece (districts as edtech creators) sits alongside the Gemini-implementation story (success depends on local PD, not vendor sales) and the AI Center of Excellence piece in higher ed. Direction of travel: districts and institutions are bringing more of the AI integration work in-house rather than buying turnkey.

4. Techlash with Accessibility Asterisk

EdSurge's piece on disability advocates worrying about exclusion is the first clearly articulated tension within the broader techlash — a reminder that "less tech" is not value-neutral when assistive tech is the access mechanism for many students.

5. Learning Impact T-6 Days

With the SaaS-risk and AI-governance threads dominating, the June 1–3 1EdTech conference in San Francisco is now the natural venue for industry-wide responses. Worth watching for any joint vendor commitments on SaaS shared responsibility or AI safety.
