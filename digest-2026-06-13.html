Executive Summary

The mood this week tilts from AI enthusiasm to AI skepticism: MIT's Justin Reich (EdTech Digest) and a new Brookings analysis both ask whether AI in education will actually deliver, while a fresh crop of pieces probes the genie that's already out of the bottle — an agentic-AI cheating crisis, classroom deepfakes, and "data you can't take back" once AI features arrive pre-enabled. Cybersecurity remains the structural backdrop, with the Canvas outage still echoing through Inside Higher Ed and EdTech Magazine pivoting to proactive frameworks (CTEM) and budget-justification ("Cybersecurity ROI"). Meanwhile the policy machinery turns: the FCC opens a "top-to-bottom" review of E-Rate, and Brookings convenes on the post-2030 global education agenda.

Key Themes & Trends

1. From AI Hype to AI Scrutiny

The week's center of gravity shifts toward hard questions: MIT's Justin Reich asks "What Schools Still Don't Know About AI," Brookings asks "Will AI in Education Succeed?," and EdSurge's Terri Taylor reframes AI as a force changing how students learn rather than replacing teachers. The optimism of adoption is giving way to a demand for evidence.

2. The Agentic-AI Threat Surface

A distinctly new worry emerges: AI agents that act. EdTech Magazine's "Agentic AI Cheating Crisis" describes bots posing as students, classroom deepfakes turn AI into a cyberbullying vector, and OpenAI's desktop "superapp" signals ever-more-capable agents arriving on student devices.

3. Data Governance & Consent

EdTech Digest's "The Data You Can't Take Back" — on pre-enabled AI features and the right to delete — pairs with THE Journal's "AI Is Moving Faster than Data Trust" survey to surface a consent-and-control problem running ahead of policy.

4. Cybersecurity Matures From Alarm to Discipline

The Canvas aftermath persists (Inside Higher Ed's "Canvas Crashes," EdSurge's recap), but the response is hardening into frameworks and budgets: CTEM for proactive risk management, "Cybersecurity ROI" for funding the work, and 1EdTech's June 25 "shared responsibility" webinar for vendor accountability.

5. Policy & Infrastructure in Motion

The FCC's "top-to-bottom" E-Rate review could reshape K–12 connectivity funding, the White House AI Security Framework sets a national tone, and Brookings' post-2030 agenda event widens the lens to global education priorities.

6. Building Human Capacity for AI

Counter to the threat narrative, Ventura College's faculty communities of practice, Inside Higher Ed's "AI Across the Curriculum," and EdTech Magazine's "Data Literacy Is Key to AI ROI" all argue the real bottleneck is people, not tools — and that capacity is built collaboratively.
