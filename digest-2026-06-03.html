Key Themes & Trends

1. Learning Impact 2026 — Closing Day

1EdTech's flagship interoperability conference wraps its final day today, June 3, in San Francisco (June 1–3). The calendar behind it stays full: EUNIS Congress (June 2–4), Engage Learning (June 9–10), the OneRoster/Ed-Fi webinar (June 17), the "shared responsibility for edtech security" webinar (June 25), a Trusted Learning Context public-comment session (July 23), CBExchange (Sep 30–Oct 3), and Learning Impact 2027 in San Antonio (Feb 8–10). The themes flooding this week's news — AI governance, data foundations, and SaaS/cybersecurity risk — map directly onto the conference floor; expect the closing day to crystallize vendor commitments on shared responsibility and interoperability.

2. AI Governance & Guardrails Move From Talk to Policy

Today's strongest fresh cluster is governance. EdSurge's June 2 report finds school IT officials are worried about AI adoption and cybersecurity at once, THE Journal's June 1 piece offers 3 concrete starting points for AI guardrails in K-12 (policy that augments rather than replaces human judgment), and EdTech Digest's "K–12 Tech Reckoning" captures what districts now demand from vendors. The conversation has shifted from "should we use AI" to "what are the rules, and who is accountable."

3. AI Readiness Starts With Data

A sharper framing emerges across EdTech Magazine: AI value is gated on data. The new K-12 piece argues "AI readiness starts with the data" (a secure foundation via Microsoft Fabric/Purview), while the higher-ed side pairs "Data Literacy Is Key to AI ROI" with "Building an AI Center of Excellence" and "Higher Education's Role in Supporting K–12 AI Literacy." The throughline: before models deliver returns, institutions need governed data and a workforce literate enough to use it.

4. Cloud, Cybersecurity & Resilience (Reinforced)

The infrastructure thread holds and hardens. EdSurge re-surfaces the Canvas attack as evidence schools still struggle with cybersecurity, EdTech Magazine adds digital resilience and cloud-outage continuity planning for K-12, and the standing "Cloud Security All Hands on Deck" and zero-trust pieces persist. 1EdTech's June 25 "shared responsibility" webinar is the natural next beat — and a likely hallway topic as Learning Impact closes.

5. Adolescent Literacy — A New Cluster

A reading thread coalesces. EdTech Digest's June 2 review of LIFT (Literacy Intervention for Teens) opens with a stark figure — 773 million teens globally struggle with basic reading — and pairs naturally with THE Journal's "AI Won't Replace Teachers… Structured Literacy at Scale" and ETI's tutoring/literacy evergreen pieces. The focus is shifting from early literacy toward the harder, often-neglected problem of adolescent readers.

6. Well-Being & Human Connection (Carryover)

The persistent counter-current continues: EdSurge's screen-time/edtech-vetting backlash and accessibility-exclusion concerns sit alongside EdTech Digest's "Where Edtech Meets Human Connection" and Inside Higher Ed's "Why Are Students Opening Up to AI Instead of People?" — keeping the human-centered case in view against the AI acceleration story.
