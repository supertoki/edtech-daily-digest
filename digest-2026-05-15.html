Key Themes & Trends

1. The CTO Pressure Cooker — Cybersecurity + AI + LMS All at Once

Inside Higher Ed's new May 12 quick-take "Survey: CTOs on Cybersecurity, AI, the LMS" reports that the 2026 Campus CTO survey finds colleges "struggle to keep pace with rapid technological change across multiple institutional priorities" — pairing with the same survey's May 12 long-form piece reporting half of campus tech leaders question AI's ROI. Combined with EdTech Magazine's K-12 enrollment-decline piece, the picture is one of compounding pressures landing on the same office at the same time.

2. Academic Integrity Flipped — "Endemic Microcheating" by Faculty

Inside Higher Ed's May 14 "Endemic Microcheating by Academics Going Unpunished" is today's most provocative piece. An education dean argues that questionable research practices among academics have been overshadowed by focus on student AI use — directly inverting the AI-integrity conversation that has dominated higher ed for two years. Worth pairing with yesterday's "Real Problem With AI in STEM Education": both reframe the AI-integrity problem as a capability problem in the people doing evaluation, not in the tools.

3. Rural AI Equity Emerges as a Distinct Frame

THE Journal's "13 School and District Teams to Participate in Rural AI Strategy Lab" (May 11) signals that the AI-equity conversation now has a distinctly rural dimension separate from urban or socioeconomic framings. Pairs with EdTech Digest's May 4 "Cybersecurity Is an Equity Issue in K-12 Education" — both arguing that the resource gap shapes which districts can responsibly adopt new tech.

4. Evidence-Based Curriculum Quietly Compounds — STEMscopes + Structured Literacy

THE Journal reports STEMscopes Math achieved ESSA Level 3 certification (May 14), the highest evidence tier. Read alongside the May 13 piece on AI + structured literacy at scale and Ed-Fi's "Early Literacy Support That Reaches Every Student", an evidence-based curriculum + AI-delivery narrative is consolidating across math and literacy.

5. ELL Recovery as the Live Edge of Pandemic Aftermath

EdSurge's "This Ohio District Is Turning the Tide" (May 13) is one of the few pieces this week putting English learner literacy recovery at the center — five years after closures, the recovery story has narrowed to specific subpopulations and specific districts.

6. Change Management & PD Return as First-Order Topics

EdTech Magazine's two new K-12 pieces — "Put a More Positive Spin on K-12 Disruptors and Change Management" and "Professional Development for IT Teams in the Age of Digital Citizenship" — both reframe the moment as a people problem, not just a technology problem. Trusted-advisor relationships and IT-team upskilling are reasserting themselves as core operational levers.

7. Classroom Infrastructure Modernization Continues — AV-over-IP

EdTech Magazine's new Higher Ed piece on AV-over-IP classroom upgrades is a reminder that under the AI/cyber headlines, physical classroom modernization is still happening on its own track — simpler installation, lower TCO, smarter rooms.

Final call to submit speaker applications for the October 13–15 NYC event at Columbia/Teachers College.
