Key Themes & Trends

1. Governance Before Deployment

EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," SUNY's systemwide AI-policy deadline (EdTech Magazine), and the HBS principal's guide all push the same sequence: policy first, procurement second. THE Journal's "AI Impact Relies on a Strong Data Foundation" makes the technical version of the same argument.

2. Cybersecurity Matures from Alarm to Operations

THE Journal's "finding flaws to fixing them," EdTech Magazine's post-Canvas-breach vendor-risk guidance, deepfake defenses, and Marshall University's cybersecurity workforce pipeline together show security shifting from crisis framing to durable practice and talent-building.

3. The AI Reality Check

The Gen-AI budget-overrun report (THE Journal), Brookings' "risks of anthropomorphic AI," and EdTech Digest's "What Schools Still Don't Know About AI" (MIT's Justin Reich) signal a more sober, evidence-seeking phase after the initial enthusiasm.

4. Credentials & Pathways Stay Contested

Faculty unions opposing Massachusetts' three-year degrees, the apprenticeship "wish list" of funding and legal barriers (Inside Higher Ed), and community colleges aligning curriculum to workforce data keep the "what counts as proof of skill" debate live.

5. AI Literacy Goes Mainstream and Hands-On

Google's "snackable and stackable" educator training, Santa Clara's "AI Kitchen" workshops, NAAIC's expansion into high schools, and "AI across the curriculum" all reflect a move from abstract policy to practical, embedded skill-building.

6. Sector Identity in Flux

ISTE+ASCD's rebrand to the International Society for Transforming Education caps a notable organizational shift, while ISTELive 26 previews the field's next gathering.
