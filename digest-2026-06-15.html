Executive Summary

Threaded through both is a steady drumbeat of practical frameworks — for responsible student AI use, faculty AI-readiness, and continuous cyber-risk management — as the field works to operationalize last week's federal AI signals.

Key Themes & Trends

1. The Skeptical Turn on AI in Education

The week's loudest signal is doubt, not hype: Brookings asks "Will AI in Education Succeed?", MIT's Justin Reich catalogs "What Schools Still Don't Know About AI," EdSurge insists "AI Won't Replace Educators," and Inside Higher Ed reports students themselves "Aren't All In on AI." The conversation has shifted from whether to adopt toward whether it works — and at what cost.

2. Agentic AI Opens a New Security Front

EdTech Magazine's "Agentic AI Cheating Crisis" — bots impersonating students — pairs with THE Journal's coverage of Windows-as-agent-platform and OpenAI's desktop "superapp" to mark agentic AI as both an institutional opportunity and an identity-security threat.

3. Data Rights and Default-On AI

EdTech Digest's "The Data You Can't Take Back" and THE Journal's "AI Is Moving Faster than Data Trust" converge on a privacy reckoning: AI features that default to on, and deletion rights that quietly stop functioning once data is used.

4. Frameworks Over Alarm

Against the skepticism, the field is building scaffolding: Inside Higher Ed's "Practical Framework to Help Students Use AI," EdTech Magazine's CTEM cyber-risk model and Ventura College's faculty AI-readiness cohorts all translate concern into repeatable practice.

5. Connectivity and Workforce Policy Resurface

The FCC's "top-to-bottom" E-Rate review puts school-connectivity funding back in play, while Brookings on community college workforce outcomes and EdSurge on the value of degrees keep the education-to-employment pipeline in focus.

6. Student Wellbeing Holds Its Ground

Deepfake cyberbullying, cellphone-management rethinks, SEL technology guidance, and EdSurge's "What My Students Deserve" keep student safety and basic needs in view alongside the AI and infrastructure debates.
