Key Themes & Trends

1. AI Skepticism Goes Mainstream

The strongest through-line is a turn toward critical scrutiny of AI's classroom role: Brookings' "Risks of Anthropomorphic AI," Inside Higher Ed's Socratic-teaching revival, EdTech Digest's blunt "EdTech Pretends to Be for Students," and the MIT-researcher caution all push back on uncritical adoption — echoing EdSurge's "begin by talking about it."

2. Govern Before You Buy

A maturing consensus says policy must precede procurement: SUNY's systemwide AI policy (Dec 2026 deadline), EdTech Digest's "Writing the Rules Before Buying the Tools," and the Harvard principal's guide all frame governance as the first move, not an afterthought.

3. The Canvas Breach Still Reverberates

Security dominates the infrastructure conversation — university vendor-risk reviews after the 275M-user Canvas breach, K-12 AI-phishing and CTEM coverage, higher-ed deepfake defenses, and 1EdTech's "shared responsibility" webinar all trace back to the breach's fallout.

4. Agentic AI Meets Academic Integrity

EdTech Magazine's "Agentic AI Cheating Crisis" marks a new front: autonomous AI agents are escalating integrity concerns beyond what plagiarism detection was built to handle, pushing IT toward technical and policy safeguards.

5. Research Reverses a Reform (Cross-Source)

In a notable convergence, both Brookings (new Kentucky study) and Inside Higher Ed question the corequisite-course consensus — a reform widely treated as settled — signaling evidence-based recalibration of a major higher-ed remediation strategy.

6. The Cost Reckoning Begins

THE Journal's warning that half of Gen AI projects could exceed budget by 2028, alongside "data readiness as AI readiness" framing, signals attention shifting from AI enthusiasm to the economics and prerequisites of actually delivering returns.
