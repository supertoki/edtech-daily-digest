Executive Summary

This week's dominant story is the agentic AI reckoning: as AI agents grow capable of impersonating students and completing coursework autonomously, higher-ed IT is grappling with an "AI cheating crisis" while MIT's Justin Reich warns schools still don't understand what they're adopting. Running alongside is a sharpening data-trust and privacy thread — surveys show AI deployment outpacing data confidence, and commentators spotlight default-on AI features and data you "can't take back." Federal-policy and infrastructure questions round out the week, with the FCC opening a top-to-bottom E-Rate review and the lingering Canvas-outage fallout keeping cybersecurity and accessibility front of mind.

Key Themes & Trends

1. The Agentic-AI Reckoning

The week's center of gravity is AI agents acting autonomously inside education. EdTech Magazine's "What Can Higher Ed IT Do About the Agentic AI Cheating Crisis?" frames identity security as the new frontier as agents impersonate students, while EdTech Digest's "What Schools Still Don't Know About AI" (MIT's Justin Reich) and Brookings' "Will AI in Education Succeed?" both counsel caution against unproven adoption.

2. Data Trust & the Privacy Bill Comes Due

THE Journal's "AI Is Moving Faster than Data Trust" and EdTech Digest's "The Data You Can't Take Back" converge on a single anxiety: default-on AI features and rapid deployment are outrunning institutions' ability to govern, audit, and reverse data collection.

3. The Canvas Outage Has a Long Tail

A spring Canvas LMS disruption keeps surfacing — EdSurge's "Latest Canvas Attack", Inside Higher Ed's "Canvas Crashes—Paper Not So Much," and 1EdTech's June 25 "Shared Responsibility" webinar all treat platform resilience and vendor accountability as live issues.

4. Cybersecurity Matures Into Strategy

Beyond incident response, security is becoming a budgeting and framework discipline: EdTech Magazine pairs "CTEM... Manage Cyber Risk in K–12" with "Cybersecurity ROI in Higher Education," signaling a shift from alarm to measurable investment.

5. Connectivity & Federal Policy

THE Journal's report that the FCC will conduct a "top-to-bottom" E-Rate review puts the foundational funding for K–12 and library broadband back in play — a reminder that equity of access underpins every AI ambition.

6. Workforce, Credentials & Human Value

EdSurge's "Why College Degrees Matter in the Age of AI," Brookings on community-college workforce outcomes, and ISTE's enduring "human problem-solving skills" thread keep asking what human capability and credentials are worth as AI reshapes work.
